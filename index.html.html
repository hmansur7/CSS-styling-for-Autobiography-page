Early Life

On May 21st, 2004, I was born in a little town in the Indian state of Gujarat. My parents, grandparents, and cousins and I all grew up together in a tiny, bustling village. Under one big roof, a tiny cluster of houses were connected by corridors and gates to form our home. This meant that I would always be around my entire family throughout the day. I loved playing sports with my cousins when I was younger; we did everything from soccer to cricket to practicing our track and field runs. One day my cousin brought a computer, and that was when I had my very first computer encounter. From that day onwards, I decided I was going to be a programmer. Eventually, in search for a better education for me and my siblings, my parents decided that it was time to leave India to begin a fruitful life in Canada.

Education Story

I formally began attending an elementary public school on May 9, 2009. I had a lot of difficulties in the beginning of my academic career. I had trouble making friends because I did not speak English well. I was unable to make a lot of friends as a result of my lack of social skills. I also struggled academically since I couldn't understand what my teachers were saying to me. I eventually began to pick up on English and learned to comprehend and speak it. Nevertheless I struggled and remained a very weak student in terms of marks. I did however make lifelong friends because I became an extroverted personality after learning English. I took a course called Introduction to Computer Science in high school. I was highly interested in the material from the very first class I took. particularly given my lifelong desire to become a programmer. I enrolled in Computer Science 2, which was the next course after Introduction to Computer Science. I did incredibly well in both subjects and during my final two years of high school, my academic career in terms of grades really took off. I enrolled at Toronto Metropolitan University in September 2022 with the goal of pursuing a bachelor's degree in computer science. I was completely unprepared for my first semester because I wasn't used to the university setting. I consequently didn't get the best grades on my tests. However, I established additional friends who I would work on significant projects with in the future. I made the decision to take things more seriously in the second semester of my first year if I wanted to succeed in becoming a programmer. As a result, I put a lot of effort into studying for my classes both during and outside of lectures. I also got to know my instructors, who did everything in their power to ensure that I learned everything I could. I had an excellent grade point average when I finished my first year. I'm a co-op student in my second year and recently became interested in web design. I am creating this website as a result!
